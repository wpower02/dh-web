GAA Clubs in Cork

St Finbarr's

About

ST Finbarr’s hurling and football club was founded in 1976. St Finbarrs are known as a very competitive and successful club in both hurling and football. They have excelled in both over the years and are continuing to do so. They wear the iconic blue jerseys with small hints of yellow.

Achievements

  • Munster senior Club Championship (4)
  • Cork senior Hurling club championship (26)
  • Hurling All-Ireland Senior Club Championship (2)
  • Munster senior Club Championship (5)
  • Cork senior football club championship (10)
  • Football All-Ireland Senior Club Championship (3)

Bishopstown Gaa club

About

The club was founded in 1957. Bishopstown stands out in Seandun Gaa for many reasons the main one being they play in a very high standard in hurling and football. They competed at both premier senior levels up to a year ago when they were relegated to senior “A” in the football championship. Bishopstown wear the purple and white colours on there jersey.

Achievements

  • Intermediate wins in both hurling and football
  • U21 wins in both
  • 3 minor football championships

Blackrock Hurling club

About

Blackrock hurling club was founded in 1883. Blackrock like a couple of other teams that are within seandun are a hurling club. No football is played within this club they have a sister club St Michaels where the football is played. The Blackrock jersey is a fairly iconic one it’s a green and yellow striped jersey.

Achievements

  • Hurling All-Ireland Senior Club Championship (3)
  • Munster senior Club Championship (5)
  • Cork senior Hurling club championship (33)
  • Cork minor hurling championship (11)

Na Phiarsaigh

About

Na Phiarsaigh Gaa club is a hurling and football club founded in 1943. Na Phiarsaigh is mainly known as a hurling stronghold. They have made many players who have many all Irelands with cork and who were a crucial part to those winning teams. Black and amber the colour of their jersey represents the club very weel.

Achievements

  • Munster senior Club Championship (17)
  • Cork senior hurling League (5)
  • Cork minor hurling championship (11)
  • Cork minor football championship (3)

    • Glen Rovers

    About

    The Glen Rovers hurling club was founded in 1916. As well as the hurling team being successful, the Glen Rovers camogie team was just as successful, proving the passion they had for the game up in the Northsides. Alike Blackrock, they also are just a hurling club with St Nicks being the football club. The facilities of the two teams are shared though unlike Blackrock and St Michaels. Their jersey would strike fear into their opponents, the infamous green, yellow, and black.

    Achievements

    • Hurling All-Ireland Senior Club Championship (2)
    • Munster senior Club Championship (3)
    • Cork senior Hurling club championship (27)
    • Cork minor hurling championship (27)

    Douglas

    About

    Douglas Hurling and football club was founded in 1887. Douglas competes at the top level in both hurling and football, each of the 2 teams play premier senior. Douglas wears the green and black striped jersey.

    Achievements

    • Premier hurling and football minor county championships
    • Cork city Junior hurling and football championships
    • Intermediate Hurling and football championships

    St Michaels

    About

    St Michaels GAA club was founded in 1951. It is the sister club of the hurling team Blackrock as a result St Michaels is just a football club. They wear the green and yellow colors, the jerseys being mostly green with a yellow stripe.

    Achievements

    • Cork Senior A Football Championship Winners 2022
    • Minor Football championship (7)
    • U21 football championship (2)

Nemo Rangers

Nemo Rangers GAA club was founded in 1922. They are predominately a football club but still name numerous juvenile hurling teams and an adults hurling team. Their jersey color shown in the image below is black and green and is known throughout the country.

Achievements

  • Football All-Ireland Senior Club Championship (7)
  • Munster senior Club Championship (17)
  • Cork senior football club championship (23)
  • Cork minor football championships (11)

UCC

UCC is a hurling and football club that stands out from the rest of the clubs in Seandun as it is the only college within. UCC is a college with a great sporting history. UCC is represented by only its students, if you are not a student you are not allowed to play. Another thing separating UCC from the rest is that if you are a student and your club plays either senior hurling or football, they are then ineligible to play for the club with the most iconic crest (the skull and crossbones).

Achievements

  • Cork senior football championship (10)
  • Cork senior hurling championship (2)
  • Munster senior football Club Championship (3)

St Vincents

St Vincents is a hurling and football club in Cork city which was founded in 1943. This club wouldn’t have the biggest pick of players being from an area with many senior teams nearby. They have done well as a club and have represented Seandun well as a club. They wear a green and white kit. The jersey being mostly white with a couple of white lines down the arms.

Achievements

  • Cork Intermediate Hurling Championship Winners (1)
  • Cork Premier Intermediate Football Championship winners (2)
  • Munster Intermediate Club Football Championship Runners-Up 2012
  • Cork Junior Football Championship Winners (1)

St Nicholas

about

St Nicholas were founded in 1901. They are the football equivalent of Glen Rovers. They wear a striped white and black jersey.

Achievements

  • Munster Senior Club Championship (1)
  • Cork Senior Football Club Championship (5)

Mayfield

about

Mayfield GAA club was founded in 1893. At the start of the 1900s, Mayfield joined with Brian Dillon and Sarsfield, both local teams, to make a senior team as the three clubs were all small at the time. However, this fell through. Mayfield wears a red and white jersey.

Achievements

  • All-Ireland Junior Club Hurling Championship (1)
  • Munster Junior Club Hurling Championship (1)
  • Cork Junior Hurling Championship (2)
  • Cork City Junior Hurling Championship (11)
